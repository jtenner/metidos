# Photosensitivity & Seizure Safety

Use this skill when evaluating motion graphics, video, transitions, ads, or other visual effects. Focus on preventing content that could trigger seizures, migraines, nausea, or severe discomfort.

## Assess Visual Risk

- Identify flashing, strobing, rapid contrast changes, or high-intensity animation
- Review full-screen effects, looping banners, GIFs, particle effects, and video content
- Consider users with photosensitive epilepsy, migraines, vestibular disorders, or sensory sensitivities
- Flag effects that cannot be paused, reduced, or avoided

## Improve Safety

- Remove or reduce high-risk flashing and rapid luminance changes
- Respect reduced-motion preferences and provide calmer alternatives
- Avoid using intense visual effects for essential information or delight features
- Give users control to pause, stop, or avoid problematic content

## Output

- Highlight seizure or sensory-trigger risks
- Recommend safer design alternatives and mitigations
- Prioritize immediate action for high-risk visual patterns
