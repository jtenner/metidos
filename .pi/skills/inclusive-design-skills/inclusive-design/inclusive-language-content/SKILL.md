# Inclusive Language & Content

Use this skill when reviewing written content, labels, and messaging. Focus on whether language is understandable, respectful, and broadly accessible across identities, literacy levels, and contexts.

## Review for Inclusion

- Identify jargon, idioms, assumptions, or exclusionary phrasing
- Check whether content reflects diverse identities and avoids unnecessary gendering or bias
- Review tone for respect, clarity, and directness
- Consider translation, plain language, and cultural context

## Improve Content

- Use specific, concrete wording that avoids ambiguity and gatekeeping
- Prefer neutral, respectful language unless specificity is required
- Make instructions and messages easy to act on
- Ensure accessibility language is accurate and non-patronizing

## Output

- Flag confusing or exclusionary wording
- Rewrite critical content in clearer, more inclusive terms
- Prioritize high-visibility and task-critical content first
