# Small-Screen Reader Optimisation

Use this skill when reviewing the experience of screen reader users on phones. Focus on reducing navigation effort, improving context, and preventing repetitive or disorienting interaction.

## Evaluate Navigation Burden

- Count how many swipes or actions are needed to reach common tasks
- Check whether controls have concise, meaningful labels in limited space
- Review repeated elements, nested groups, and long unstructured screens
- Identify hidden context caused by collapsed sections, tabs, or visual-only grouping

## Improve Efficiency

- Chunk content into shorter sections with clear headings and landmarks
- Make actions easy to distinguish when announced sequentially
- Reduce redundant announcements and excessive wrapper elements
- Ensure navigation, search, and filtering are reachable without excessive traversal

## Output

- Describe where small-screen screen reader use becomes inefficient or confusing
- Recommend structural or labeling changes that reduce effort
- Focus on improvements for frequent and high-value user journeys
