# Content Structure & Hierarchy

Use this skill when reviewing how information is grouped, ordered, and presented. Focus on making structure clear both visually and semantically so users can quickly understand what matters and how content relates.

## Review Structure

- Check page titles, headings, sections, grouping, and sequence of content
- Identify flat or inconsistent layouts that obscure relationships or priority
- Consider scanning, comprehension, and navigation for screen reader and cognitive accessibility
- Review whether key actions and supporting details are placed logically

## Improve Hierarchy

- Use clear headings and meaningful grouping to show content relationships
- Keep information architecture predictable across pages and states
- Align visual hierarchy with semantic structure and task flow
- Reduce fragmentation, duplication, and hidden dependencies between sections

## Output

- Highlight structural confusion and weak hierarchy
- Recommend clearer grouping, ordering, and emphasis
- Prioritize improvements for content-heavy and task-critical experiences
