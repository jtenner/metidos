# Motion, Haptics & Feedback

Use this skill when reviewing mobile feedback and transitions. Focus on whether motion and haptic behavior support comprehension without causing discomfort, distraction, or overload.

## Review Sensory Effects

- Identify animations, parallax, auto-playing motion, and gesture-driven transitions
- Review haptic feedback frequency, intensity, and reliance for confirmation
- Check whether motion is essential to understanding or merely decorative
- Consider users with vestibular disorders, sensory sensitivities, or attention challenges

## Improve Feedback

- Respect reduced-motion preferences and provide calmer alternatives
- Use haptics as enhancement, not the only signal
- Keep transitions short, predictable, and non-disorienting
- Pair feedback with clear visual or textual confirmation where appropriate

## Output

- Highlight motion or haptic patterns that may exclude users
- Recommend safer, multi-modal feedback strategies
- Prioritize fixes where discomfort or missed confirmation affects task success
