# Audio & Video Accessibility

Use this skill when evaluating recorded or live media experiences. Focus on whether users can perceive, control, and understand audio/video content across hearing, vision, cognitive, and situational needs.

## Review Media Access

- Check captions, transcripts, audio descriptions, and speaker/context clarity
- Review playback controls for keyboard, screen reader, and touch accessibility
- Identify autoplay, inaccessible players, or media that blocks task completion
- Consider users in noisy, quiet, low-bandwidth, or attention-limited contexts

## Improve Media Experience

- Provide accurate captions and transcripts for meaningful spoken content
- Ensure important visual information is described or otherwise conveyed
- Make controls discoverable, operable, and understandable
- Avoid forcing users to rely on sound or vision alone for essential information

## Output

- Highlight missing media accommodations and control issues
- Recommend practical improvements for perception and operability
- Prioritize content or player fixes that affect key journeys
