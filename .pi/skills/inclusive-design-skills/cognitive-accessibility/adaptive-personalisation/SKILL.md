# Adaptive Personalisation

Use this skill when designing experiences that can flex to support different cognitive profiles. Focus on giving users meaningful control over how they receive, process, and act on information.

## Personalisation Opportunities

- Identify where users may benefit from changing text density, contrast, spacing, timing, or guidance
- Look for rigid flows that force one pace, one format, or one level of detail
- Consider needs related to ADHD, autism, dyslexia, memory impairments, anxiety, or brain injury
- Prioritize preferences that reduce overwhelm and improve confidence

## Design for User Control

- Offer clear settings or contextual toggles for simplification, reminders, and sensory reduction
- Let users pause, resume, review, and customize notifications or interruptions
- Preserve functionality across modes; do not treat personalization as a lesser experience
- Make preferences easy to discover, understand, and persist over time

## Output

- Suggest where personalisation would reduce cognitive friction
- Recommend concrete controls or modes aligned with user needs
- Highlight areas where inflexible defaults create unnecessary exclusion
