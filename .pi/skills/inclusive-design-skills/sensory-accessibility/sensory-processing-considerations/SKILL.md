# Sensory Processing Considerations

Use this skill when evaluating whether an experience is overstimulating or difficult to tolerate. Focus on cumulative sensory load across visuals, motion, sound, density, and interruptions.

## Assess Sensory Load

- Review animation, sound, contrast, clutter, brightness, and competing stimuli
- Identify auto-playing media, persistent notifications, or dense, noisy layouts
- Consider users with autism, ADHD, migraines, PTSD, or sensory processing differences
- Look for spaces where multiple mild stressors combine into overload

## Reduce Overwhelm

- Simplify visual presentation and reduce competing motion or noise
- Offer calm modes, reduced-motion options, and user control over alerts or media
- Make tasks predictable and avoid sudden sensory changes
- Preserve core functionality in lower-stimulation variants

## Output

- Highlight overstimulating patterns and cumulative burden
- Recommend changes that create calmer, more tolerable experiences
- Prioritize improvements in high-frequency and high-stress flows
