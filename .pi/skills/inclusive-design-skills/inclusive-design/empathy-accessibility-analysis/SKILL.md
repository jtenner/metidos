# Empathy & Accessibility Analysis

Use this skill when stepping through a product as if you were a disabled user in context. Focus on the lived experience: effort, confidence, frustration, dependency, and whether the flow feels respectful and usable.

## Analyze the Journey

- Walk through the task from the user’s likely environment and constraints
- Identify moments of uncertainty, overload, interruption, or exclusion
- Consider emotional impact: embarrassment, anxiety, fatigue, loss of trust, or abandonment
- Look beyond isolated screens to end-to-end friction

## Turn Insight into Action

- Translate pain points into concrete design or content improvements
- Distinguish between inconvenience and true barriers to completion
- Note where assumptions about ability or context break the experience
- Emphasize dignity, autonomy, and confidence in recommendations

## Output

- Describe likely user experience in practical, human terms
- Highlight the most harmful friction points and why they matter
- Recommend changes that improve usability and respect across the journey
